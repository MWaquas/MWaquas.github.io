Redesign of Bahen Courtyard

Engineering Design / Sustainability / Project Management

← Back to portfolio
Overview

This project focused on redesigning the 1,250 m² Bahen Courtyard at the University of Toronto St. George campus to create a sustainable, accessible, and multifunctional gathering space with food access.

Design Context

The client for this project was the University of Toronto Sustainability Office. The redesign was constrained by existing infrastructure, including rainwater cistern towers, a water feature, and mature trees, all of which were required to remain in place. Additional constraints included weather conditions, fire safety regulations, noise limits, and accessibility requirements.

Design Process

The team identified key stakeholders, service environment conditions, and detailed functions, objectives, and constraints (FOCs). A structured ideation process was used, generating 64 initial concepts through brainstorming and morph charts.

These concepts were systematically narrowed to three viable alternatives using feasibility checks, multi-voting, graphical decision charts, and a Pugh matrix to select a final conceptual design.

Outcome

The final conceptual design meets sustainability, accessibility, and operational objectives outlined by the client. The complete design documentation received a 96% evaluation and is currently under consideration for future implementation by the university.
