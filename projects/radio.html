Overview

This project focuses on designing and implementing a high-frequency RF transmitter power amplifier & filter. The radio operates between 8–16 MHz. The system delivers up to 1-10W of power, and attenuates harmonics.

*** IMPORTANT NOTE ***
This project is still in development, and is projected to be completed by April 30, 2026.

Design Motivation

The goal of this design was to build a complete RF transmit sub-system that can amplify low-power signals to usable levels while maintaining signal quality. This involved addressing key challenges such as power delivery, harmonic suppression, and impedance matching within the 8–16 MHz range.

The project also served as a practical application of RF and analog circuit design principles, and is the main focus of a course I am taking at the University of Toronto.

Ideation

The design process began with brainstorming multiple approaches for amplifying and transmitting the RF signal. Different amplifier topologies, including linear and switching designs, were considered based on efficiency, complexity, and performance requirements. Component options and circuit configurations were evaluated through datasheets and reference designs to determine feasibility.

A Class D switching amplifier was ultimately selected due to its high efficiency and suitability for the target frequency range. Figure 1 shows the electrical schematic of the design.

Figure 1. Image shows the electrical schematic of the design.
LTspice Design & Validation

The circuit was first developed and validated in LTspice to ensure it met performance requirements before hardware implementation. Over 20 simulations were used to analyze output power, frequency response, and harmonic distortion across the 8–16 MHz range. Results showed the system achieved the objective of 1-10W of output power with low total harmonic distortion, confirming the effectiveness of the amplifier and filtering stages. PCB Design

Following simulation, the system was implemented as a PCB using Altium Designer. The design involved schematic capture, component selection, and layout optimization for signal integrity and power routing. Datasheets were used to ensure accurate footprint creation and proper component integration. The final PCB reflects key considerations such as high-frequency behavior, component placement, and overall manufacturability. Figures 4, and 5 shows the PCB.
